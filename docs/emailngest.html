REQUEST FOR EXTENSION OF AUTHORIZATION TO MAKE CASH PAYMENTS IN EXCESS OF USD 500 (ETJ AND ETK)

 

(U) 1. Ethiopia remains primarily a cash economy. Therefore, post must continue to pay some vendors in local currency.

(U) 2. The Embassy Cashier continues to collect from the Consular Section an average of 10,000 in local currency and USD $1,000 each business day. Making some cash payments also helps reduce cash on hand and keep the Class B Cashiers accountability within limits.  The two U.S. Missions in Addis Ababa rely heavily on cash and checks to conduct local transactions. In the current environment, cash payments are not only necessary for routine petty cash transactions, but for many of our certified vouchers. Routine payments in excess of $500 include utilities, facility maintenance and repair supplies, telephone bills, limited vendor payments, U.S. Citizens repatriation loans, limited DOD & PAS invitational travel advances and customs clearances.

(U) 3. The number of cash payments greater than USD 500 for the period April 1, 2023, and March 31, 2024, were 1,069 for both cashiers (ETJ and ETK). Post will continue its efforts to reduce cash payments.

(U) 4. Post requests authorization to continue cash payments in excess of $500, as authorized in Reftel (23 State 38592), beyond the current date of April 19, 2024.
